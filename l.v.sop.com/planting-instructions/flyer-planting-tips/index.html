Flyer Planting Tips

 

Tips for Successful Planting from the “Tree” Brothers

 

Be sure to watch our info videos on our website for easy to follow instructions

 

 

 

Warranty coverage requires that you view videos, planting instructions and watering instructions 

 

 

 

 

 

   Water your plants well when you get home if the surface is dry to touch.

 

   Water plastic pots daily until ready to plant, burlapped root balls need to be kept very moist, always do a drainage

 

   test before planting and install an inspection pipe with large plants – see info video.

 

   Plant in the late evening if possible, do not plant during hot sun.

 

   Do not plant within salt spray distance from roads

 

          or in low-lying area where water builds. Most plants do not want to sit in water.  

 

          Dig a hole twice the volume as the pot.

 

          Do not remove plants from paper pots. Cut some vertical slits every 2 inches.

 

   Remove plants from plastic pots. Wait until the last second to remove from pots. This will reduce shock to roots.

 

          Dig a few inches deeper than the dirt level in pot. Original dirt level from pot should be 1” higher or more than surrounding ground.

 

          Use quality triple mix mixed with original soil. Maximum 50% triple mix. Do not use 3-way mix containing manure with cedars, it will burn the roots, use only compost 3-way mix.

 

          Do not place fertilizer under or around sides of roots. Use fertilizer during the second and later years, never during the first year. Use      

 

          only quality commercial grade time release fertilizer. 

 

          If you see roots wrapping around the outside of dirtball that is removed from a plastic pot then the roots must be massaged and loosened             

 

          otherwise roots will keep growing in a circle and choke the plant. Rolling the dirtball while still in the pot will help loosen the roots or   

 

          rub the roots with tips of fingers or even rip them apart with a screwdriver if necessary.

 

          Place dirtball into hole, avoiding exposing roots to sun.

 

  Place new soil mixture around and under pot. Soak area well and push dirt down to eliminate air pockets. Add dirt and water in layers to      

 

  eliminate air pockets. Do not place new dirt or mulch against trunk of plant to avoid bark rot.

 

         Soak well again. Press down gently on soil to eliminate air pockets. Use hands and water to press down on soil, not feet.

 

         Until roots get established, kept dirt moist, especially during hot weather. If in doubt, ask for guidance. 

 

         Once established it is better to soak plants well every couple of days rather than light watering every day because the water won’t get to    

 

         the bottom of the roots. Allow soil to drain completely before watering again. Always wet is not good and may cause root rot or at the

 

         very least, lazy roots.  

 

         If ground is naturally moist due to temperature, rain, etc. there is no need to water. Very little if any watering is usually needed for    

 

        established plants until late May or early June.  Large plants should be watered less often but very slow and long to reach the lower roots.

 

        15 gallons applied over half an hour is about right for a large 10 foot tree. Sprinkler systems do a very poor job of watering shrubs or trees  

 

        as they usually only penetrate the surface and don’t get down deep to the bottom of the roots. Do not rely on sprinkler systems.

 

        Only water late in the evening or very early morning. Never water leaves.

 

        Over or under watering can cause the same unhealthy looking plants.

 

        Add a few inches of  real cedar mulch on top of soil, keep away from trunk.

 

        Watch leaves for signs of over or under-watering. They often look the same. Contact us immediately at first sign of colour changing on    

 

        even just one plant. This may be the sign of trouble for the rest of your plants. Failure to contact us immediately may result in

 

        invalidation of the warranty on the remaining plants.

 

 

 

Do not prune in fall, true for most plants. Always remove “suckers” from standard (grafted) plants or they will kill the “guest” plant.

 

Winterize delicate plants, rose of sharon, butterfly bushes, holly, cotoneaster, etc. Place several inches of real cedar mulch. Never leave plants in pots – plants not planted in the ground are not covered by warranty.

 

Water evergreens very heavy and long just before winter.

 

Jump on the Internet to get more specific info on your plant and pruning tips.
